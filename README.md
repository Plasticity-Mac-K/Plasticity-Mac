## 🔍 Overview

<a href="#plasticity">Plasticity</a> serves the growing segment of independent product designers, concept artists, and industrial designers who need professional NURBS CAD capability but find enterprise CAD tools prohibitively expensive, overly complex, or poorly suited to creative modeling workflows.

The <a href="#plasticity">indie product designer use case</a> covers designers who create consumer products, packaging, accessories, and physical objects for manufacturing but who are not working within large engineering organizations that standardize on SolidWorks or CATIA. Plasticity provides the geometric quality that physical product design requires at a fraction of the enterprise CAD cost. The <a href="#plasticity">concept artist workflow</a> uses Plasticity's fast modeling approach for creating hard-surface design concepts — vehicles, weapons, electronics, props — where clean NURBS surfaces produce the quality that game industry, film industry, and digital media concept design requires.

The <a href="#plasticity">Blender user pipeline</a> makes Plasticity particularly compelling for the large Blender user community — Plasticity handles the precision CAD modeling that Blender's polygon tools approach awkwardly, while Blender handles the rendering, animation, and artistic work that Plasticity doesn't address. The two tools complement each other through the Blender bridge. <a href="#plasticity">Indie Studio and Enterprise pricing tiers</a> provide accessible entry points for individual creators and team licensing for studios.

<a href="#plasticity">Section analysis view</a> cuts through solid models to inspect internal geometry and wall thicknesses — verifying that complex forms have the internal structure the design intends before committing to manufacturing preparation. <a href="#plasticity">Face and edge selection workflow</a> manipulates solid bodies at the face and edge level — the direct modeling style that distinguishes modern CAD from history-tree-dependent parametric tools.

<a href="#plasticity">Active development by Nick Bebber</a> delivers regular feature additions responding to the product design community's requests — Plasticity has grown substantially since its launch with features driven directly by user feedback. <a href="#plasticity">Growing tutorial ecosystem</a> with community-contributed and official tutorials makes learning Plasticity accessible for users from both polygon modeling and traditional CAD backgrounds.

## 💬 FAQ

<details>
<summary>Does Plasticity require prior CAD experience?</summary>

Some CAD understanding helps with solid modeling concepts. Plasticity's interface is more approachable than enterprise CAD tools. The tutorial ecosystem covers learning for users from both polygon and CAD backgrounds.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I use Plasticity for 3D printing?</summary>

Yes. Plasticity exports solid geometry suitable for 3D printing preparation. Export to OBJ or STEP for use in slicing and preparation software.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What are the differences between Indie and Enterprise licenses?</summary>

Check the current Plasticity website for the feature and usage differences between licensing tiers.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Plasticity support boolean operations between multiple bodies?</summary>

Yes. Boolean union, subtract, and intersect operations work between solid bodies in Plasticity.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Is Plasticity regularly updated?</summary>

Yes. Plasticity receives regular updates from active development by Nick Bebber with features added based on community feedback.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can Plasticity export to Rhino?</summary>

STEP format exports work with Rhino and other professional CAD applications.

</details>
